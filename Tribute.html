Life of our author Enid Blyton

  • Enid was born on 11 August 1897 ,London United Kingdom but a few days later she almost died of whooping cough.
  • Enid joined St Christopher's School in Beckenham.
  • Enid entered Arthur Mee's children's poetry competition
  • In 1915 Enid moved out of the family home to live with her friend Mary Attenborough.
  • In 1916 Enid enrolled in a National Froebel Union teacher training course at the school
  • She completed her teacher training course in December 1918.
  • In 1920, she moved to Southernhay.
  • Blyton's first book, Child Whispers, was published in 1922.
  • In July 1923, Blyton published Real Fairies.
  • The Secret Island, was published in 1938.
  • Enid published her initial book in the Amelia Jane series, Naughty Amelia Jane!
  • In 1940 Blyton published two books : Three Boys and a Circus and Children of Kidillin.
  • In 1942 Blyton released the first book in the Mary Mouse series, Mary Mouse and the Dolls' House.
  • The same year, Blyton published the first novel in the Famous Five series, Five on a Treasure Island.
  • In 1943 she published The Children's Life of Christ.
  • In 1947 Enid published Five on Kirrin Island Again.
  • The Rockingdown Mystery, was published in 1949.
  • She completed the sixth and final book of the Malory Towers series, Last Term at Malory Towers, in 1951
  • In 1963, Enid publishing the last books of The Famous Five (Five Are Together Again)
  • After a few years, Enid was admitted to a Hampstead nursing home where she died in November 1968, aged 71.
